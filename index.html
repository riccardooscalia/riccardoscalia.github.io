Global Rates Signal Lab: Duration, Carry, and Cross-Market Allocation  2026
Built with a colleague a multi-country fixed income research project to test systematic duration timing and cross-market allocation signals across Japan, the US, the UK, and Germany. Implemented bond momentum and carry signals (defined as the 10Y minus 1M yield spread), combined under simple risk controls. Performance evaluated in yield-space P&L, with an optional FX-hedged version incorporating monthly carry from interest-rate differentials. Results decomposed by country, portfolio aggregation, and bull vs bear regimes based on 10Y yield changes.
Tools: Excel, J.P. Morgan Investment Strategies No. 74 (McGorain, "Simple rules to trade duration").
Outcome: Reusable research pipeline with attribution and diagnostics to compare signal effectiveness across regimes.
