Projects

Global Rates Signal Lab: Duration, Carry, and Cross-Market Allocation (2026)
Built with my best friend and colleague a multi-country fixed income research project to test systematic duration timing and cross-market allocation signals across Japan, the US, the UK, and Germany. Implemented bond momentum and carry defined as the 10Y minus 1M yield spread, and combined signals under simple risk controls.
Performance was evaluated in yield-space P&L, with an optional FX-hedged version to include monthly carry from interest-rate differentials. Results were decomposed by country, portfolio aggregation, and bull vs bear regimes based on 10Y yield changes. Tools: Excel, J.P. Morgan Investment Strategies No.74 (McGorain, "Simple rules to trade duration").
Outcome: Produced a reusable research pipeline with attribution and diagnostics to compare signal effectiveness and robustness across regimes.
